Good to Know Before Visiting Montreal

Montreal is a friendly and relaxed city, but it has a few particular rules and habits. These simple tips can help visitors fit in more easily and avoid small surprises.

1. Be ready for French and English

Montreal is part of the French-speaking province of Quebec, so you will see French everywhere: on street signs, menus and in shops. Most people also speak English, especially in tourist areas, so you can switch between the two languages easily.

2. Driving rules are a bit different

On the Island of Montreal, turning right at a red light is not allowed. Traffic lights can also flash green to show you have priority for turning, so pay close attention to road signs.

3. Explore the underground city

Downtown Montreal has a huge underground network of tunnels, shops and metro stations. It is perfect for moving around in winter while staying warm and dry.

4. Taxes and tipping on the bill

Prices rarely include tax. Two taxes will be added at checkout, and it is normal to leave a 15–20% tip in restaurants for good service.
